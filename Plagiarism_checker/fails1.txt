In an economy, a currency is a medium of exchange that allows you to make a variety of transactions. 
Throughout history, the currency has had different forms, such as barley, gold and silver.
 Today's currency is much more regulated because it is physically made in the form of paper banknotes or coins.
 The value of these currencies is guaranteed by government promises. Although physical currencies (banknotes, coins)
 are often used on a daily basis, most large currency exchange transactions are conducted electronically.
